The Network Architect

Fibby Kioria represents a unique strategic asset for heritage organizations seeking to transform cultural knowledge into professionally compelling creative presentations while maintaining complete ownership of their narratives. As Founder and CEO of Muci Pictures, she has established herself as a premier practitioner of continental cultural stewardship—building professional creative capacity within African communities while ensuring narrative control remains firmly in local hands across diverse cultural and political contexts.

Mucii Pictures Leadership

As Founder and CEO of Mucii Pictures, she manages year-long development programs for emerging filmmakers from Ethiopia, Tanzania, Uganda, Kenya, Rwanda, and Ghana, demonstrating her ability to coordinate complex creative development processes across multiple countries while maintaining consistent quality standards and cultural sensitivity across diverse African contexts. Through Mucii Pictures, she has managed substantial international funding programs, development of continental creative infrastructure, and facilitation of complex cross-cultural creative collaborations spanning East, West, and Southern Africa.

DW Akademie Excellence

As project manager for the DW Akademie Film Development Fund, she successfully managed €227,000 in funding across the Eastern African region from April 2023 to March 2024 with zero audit queries, illustrating her exceptional capacity to manage substantial international resources while ensuring that creative decision-making authority remains with local practitioners.

Continental Infrastructure Development

Through her co-founding of the African Film Festivals Platform in early 2025, she has created sustainable creative infrastructure that serves the entire African continent rather than simply managing individual projects. The EAccelerate Fund that emerged from this work provides grants up to €20,000 combined with comprehensive mentorship and international distribution opportunities, creating a complete development pathway that supports creative practitioners from across Africa from initial concept through market access.

Global Bridge-Building

Her service on film festival juries worldwide and selection committees for Hot Docs Canada, Chicken & Egg Films USA, and Locarno Open Doors positions her as a crucial bridge between African filmmaking and international funding and distribution opportunities, ensuring authentic African stories find global platforms without losing their cultural specificity. Her extensive work across Eastern, Western, and Southern Africa, combined with her board positions with organizations spanning Uganda, Nigeria, and Kenya, has established her as a continental connector within African creative networks.

Strategic Partnerships & Current Initiatives

As Vice President and Head of Development at Beware of Time, she works directly with acclaimed filmmaker Ntare Guma Mbaho Mwine to champion African storytelling on a global stage while actively scouting for compelling projects from across the continent. Her co-creation of the inaugural Return to the Source Film Residency in Namibia brought together artists from Namibia, Ethiopia, Tanzania, and Kenya to explore colonial history and trauma through collaborative artistic processes. This residency demonstrates her sophisticated understanding of how to create productive creative environments where sensitive cultural material can be explored through collaborative artistic processes without compromising local agency, while simultaneously building connections between African practitioners from different regions.

Return to the Source Residency

The residency brought together artists from Namibia, Ethiopia, Tanzania, and Kenya to explore colonial history and trauma, but the program structure ensured that local community members maintained creative authority over their own stories while benefiting from knowledge exchange with practitioners from other African contexts. Participating artists contributed their technical skills and creative techniques while local participants retained complete ownership of their cultural narratives and creative vision. This approach provides a replicable model for heritage organizations seeking to engage with European partners in ways that enhance rather than extract local cultural capacity, while building stronger connections within the continental African creative ecosystem.

Durban International Film Festival 2025

As lead facilitator for the Durban International Film Festival's 2025 residency program, she designed and implemented a comprehensive program that strengthens existing local capacity while building connections to broader continental and international networks, working with eight festival directors from across South Africa. Her approach to this residency illustrates her sophisticated understanding of professional development for cultural organizations that operates across different African regional contexts. Rather than imposing external expertise, she has created intensive skill-building workshops combined with personalized development and strategic network building that allows participants to strengthen their own local festivals while sharing their unique perspectives with broader continental and international audiences.

Note: Fibby previously served as Program Director of Maisha Film Lab (2012-2021), where she organized an average of eight film labs per annum and oversaw the funding and production of film projects across East Africa, Ethiopia, and Ghana. This foundational experience in continental film development continues to inform her current work at Mucii Pictures and broader initiatives. Her eight-year tenure provided unparalleled experience in talent development across multiple African countries, creating the operational expertise that makes her current continental infrastructure work so effective.
