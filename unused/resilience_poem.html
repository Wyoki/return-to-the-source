The Journey of Resilience

A testament to survival, strength, and the enduring spirit of African communities

Not there yet, but we are here! The journey was perilous but somehow we arrived, lessons we derived, of how to hypothetically thrive, but things on the ground are different. You respect the grind but the feeling isn't always mutual, came up short as usual, but it's all about the long game. Haven't seen success yet so I belabor the merits of failure, embody the tenets of resilience. I'm, we are still here, somehow we survived, deprived, somehow we revived the teachings of our ancestors recognizing that the answer may only breed more questions, like what next, why now, are we there yet? In the Context of Return to the Source

This poem reflects the resilience that defines our work at Return to the Source. Through trauma-informed filmmaking and cultural reclamation, we honor the enduring strength of African communities while building pathways for future generations.
