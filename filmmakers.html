Filmmakers & Program

Meet the creative minds and program details from our Pan-African Film Lab & Residency in Namibia

Meet the Filmmakers

Cece Mlay

Tanzania

Filmmaker currently at Kijiweni Productions working as Assistant Director and Creative Supervisor. Her award-winning work includes "Vuta N'kuvute" and "Apostles of Cinema," exploring social, political, and historical issues. Her directorial feature debut "The Empty Grave" is currently in development.

Hiwot Admasu

Ethiopia

Ethiopian filmmaker who has made several short films since 2015. She draws inspiration from Ethiopian Orthodox Church traditions and studied electrical engineering before discovering her passion for film.

Saitabao Kaiyare

Kenya

Filmmaker with a decade of experience in Africa and Europe. His short films, documentaries, and TV shows have garnered critical acclaim at international festivals. He lectures at Pwani University and is co-founder of Baruu Collective.

Shili Munyama

Namibia

Visual artist, creative director, and filmmaker based in Windhoek. Leads Creative South, a production company creating work that captures African youth culture. His debut feature "Wrong Generation" is set for release in 2024.

Esther Buekes

South Africa

Video content producer with 12 years of experience across Africa. Her short film "Miss Understood" explores mental health and has been recognized at various festivals. She is also the current chairperson of the Namibia Film Commission.

Lindiwe Ngwenya

Namibia

Beginner filmmaker pursuing honors in Media Studies at University of Namibia. Her experience includes video projects and podcasts, with a keen interest in exploring Namibian history for diverse audiences.

Tekla Naango

Namibia

Visionary filmmaker from Opuwo, Kunene Region. Uses film to preserve cultural heritage and tell stories that transcend boundaries. Blends tradition and modernity to create compelling narratives that resonate universally.

Hilma Sheehama

Namibia

Writer, director, and award-winning production designer with 10 years of experience. Holds a diploma in Television Production and a scholarship from the Multichoice Talent Factory. Won Best Production Design at the Botswana Film Festival.

Veripuami Nandee

Namibia

Poet and journalist, winner of the 2023 Bank Windhoek Doek Literary Award. Her poetry collection "The Loneliness of Shadows" was a Top 6 Finalist in The Kinsman's Quarterly Diaspora Award. Published in various African literary magazines.

Annina Van Neel

Namibia

Founder of The Tiekie Box Project and environmental activist. Her documentary "A Story of Bones" chronicles her work excavating African burial grounds on Saint Helena Island. Uses film to engage descendant communities on heritage and memory.

Kalundu Leslie

Namibia

Second-year media student at University of Namibia pursuing a career in film and broadcast. Has written several film scripts and established his own production company. Committed to bringing his vision to life in Namibia's media landscape.

Justin Hango

Namibia

BA in Motion Picture & Live Performance from Stella Adler Los Angeles. Producer, director, and actor at Illusions of Grandeur Namibia. Brings marketing and brand building expertise to the film industry.

Meet the Facilitators

Dr. Nashilongweshipwe Mushaandja

Performance Scholar & Cultural Worker

Resident cultural worker, educator, and writer at Owela Live Arts Collective Trust since 2014. As a performance artist, his practice focuses on African performance archives and public cultures of social movements. Holds a PhD in Performance Studies from the University of Cape Town and teaches at the University of Namibia.

Khalid Shamis

Documentary Director & Editor

Documentary director, producer, and editor with a preference for editing. Doctoral fellow at the Centre for Humanities Research at the University of the Western Cape and director of Rough Cut Lab Africa, the only lab dedicated to supporting story construction for African documentaries on the continent.

Perivi Katjavivi

Lead Facilitator & Filmmaker

Distinguished Namibian filmmaker whose films have premiered at major festivals including Berlinale, Rotterdam, and Busan. His recent feature "Under the Hanging Tree" was Namibia's first Academy Awards submission. Currently pursuing a PhD in History at the University of the Western Cape, researching colonial memory sites.

Fibby Kioria

Program Director & Producer

Accomplished producer with over thirty short films and founder of Mucii Pictures. In 2018, she co-founded Manyatta Screenings, an online and outdoor short film festival in Kenya. Founding member of the East African Screen Collective advocating for narrative sovereignty in screen-based sectors.

Merapi Gottlieb Kazombiaze

Community Historian & Educator

Herero historian and teacher from Okandjira, Namibia, dedicated to preserving the memory of the Herero Genocide. Raised by his grandmother, a survivor of the 1904 German colonial massacres. Has taught in his community for 38 years and maintains connections with Germany through friends and family.

Program Overview

The residency unfolded over five days, each with a distinct focus but tied together by the core methodology of working with history as a living archive. The program combined historical site visits, oral testimony, masterclasses, outdoor screenings, and collaborative filmmaking.
