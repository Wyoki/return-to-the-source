Return to the Source

Transformative African Cinema Leadership

A groundbreaking Pan-African film lab that bridges history, healing, and future narratives through the convergence of three extraordinary cultural leaders. We redefine film education as both artistic expression and therapeutic intervention, creating a transformative approach to confronting colonial legacies and reclaiming African stories.

Perivi Katjavivi

Visionary Bridge-Builder & Cinema Scholar

Academic Foundation

PhD Candidate: History, University of the Western Cape

MA: African Cinema, University of Cape Town

BA: Cinema, Columbia College Hollywood

Cultural Position

Son of Namibian political leader Peter Katjavivi and British mother Jane Katjavivi, positioning him as a unique cultural bridge-builder in African cinema.

Key Achievement

Directed "Under the Hanging Tree" - Namibia's first-ever Academy Award submission, establishing Namibian cinema on the global stage.

The Philosophy Architect

Perivi John Katjavivi occupies a unique position in African cinema as both a bridge between cultures and a chronicler of post-colonial African experiences. His bicultural heritage positions him as uniquely equipped to navigate both African storytelling traditions and international film markets without compromising authenticity.

Revolutionary Academic Research

His groundbreaking PhD thesis on the contribution of Namibian cinema to new post-colonial identity directly informs the lab's approach to using film as a tool for cultural reclamation and healing. This academic rigor distinguishes the initiative from purely practical filmmaking workshops, embedding each creative exercise within broader historical and cultural contexts.

"Return to the Source" Methodology

Drawing inspiration from Amilcar Cabral's revolutionary framework and African filmmakers like Souleymane Cissé, he has developed a distinctive approach that views precolonial African epistemologies not as historical curiosities but as practical resources for addressing contemporary challenges. His work demonstrates how indigenous knowledge systems can be authentically integrated into contemporary narrative forms.

Cultural Innovation Through Cinema

His integration of Herero mythology and the symbolism of the Omumborombonga tree in local culture demonstrates how traditional wisdom can inform cutting-edge creative practice. This approach teaches emerging filmmakers to develop "a new cinematic language for the future" that honors ancestral knowledge while engaging contemporary audiences.

International Recognition & Impact

Katjavivi's achievement of directing Namibia's first-ever submission to the Academy Awards represents more than individual success—it signals the emergence of Namibian cinema on the global stage and establishes a template for how African filmmakers can achieve international recognition while remaining deeply rooted in local cultural contexts.

Co-founder: Manyatta Screenings (2018)

International Recognition

Festival Juries: FESPACO, Durban IFF, Zanzibar IFF, Hot Docs Canada

2025 Role: Lead Facilitator, Durban International Film Festival Residency

The Network Architect

Fibby Kioria's career represents one of the most comprehensive approaches to African film industry development on the continent. Her eight-year tenure as Program Director of Maisha Film Lab provided her with unparalleled experience in talent development across multiple African countries, organizing an average of eight film labs annually.

Continental Film Development

During her Maisha Film Lab tenure, she directly oversaw funding and production of film projects across East Africa (Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda) extending to Ethiopia and Ghana. This experience created the operational expertise that makes "Return to the Source" logistically feasible while maintaining high artistic standards.

Revolutionary Distribution Models

Through Manyatta Screenings, co-founded with Hawa Essuman, she pioneered innovative approaches to African film exhibition. The program focuses on content from greater Eastern Africa showcased in rural counties, demonstrating commitment to ensuring African films reach African audiences rather than existing primarily for international festival circuits.

Strategic Pan-African Vision

As co-founder of the African Film Festival Platform and through establishing Mucii Farmhouse as an artist-in-residence space in Kenya's savanna, she demonstrates commitment to building infrastructure that supports African film culture. Her current role as Vice President and Head of Development at Beware of Time ensures "Return to the Source" graduates have pathways to professional development.

Global Bridge-Building

Her service on film festival juries worldwide and selection committees for Hot Docs Canada, Chicken & Egg Films USA, and Locarno Open Doors positions her as a crucial bridge between African filmmaking and international funding and distribution opportunities, ensuring authentic African stories find global platforms without losing their cultural specificity.

Marcella Katjijova

Healing Architect & Trauma-Informed Guide

Cultural Authority

Heritage: Descendant of Ovaherero genocide survivors

Role: Head of Intergenerational Trauma and Recovery, Ovaherero Genocide Foundation

Professional Expertise

Registration: Psychological Counselor

Specialization: Trauma-informed care, addiction prevention, intergenerational trauma

Global Recognition

2025 Appointment: Reference Group Member, Global Cultural Assembly, Berlin's Humboldt Forum

Focus: Advancing trauma-informed care in cultural spaces

The Cultural Healer

Marcella Katjijova's identity as a descendant of Ovaherero genocide survivors provides the cultural authority essential for "Return to the Source" to authentically address historical trauma. Her dual role as professional counselor and community cultural leader ensures the lab's approach maintains appropriate cultural protocols while providing genuine therapeutic benefit.

Clinical Innovation in Cultural Context

As a registered Psychological Counselor with specialized training in trauma-informed care, she brings clinical expertise that transforms "Return to the Source" from purely artistic education into genuine therapeutic intervention. Her systematic approach to understanding how historical trauma manifests in contemporary behavioral patterns provides the framework for safe processing of difficult material.

Community Mental Health Pioneer

Through her YouTube channel and Facebook page "Paradigm Shift with Marcella," she has pioneered approaches to community mental health education that reach people unable to access traditional counseling services. This innovation directly informs the "Return to the Source" methodology, making healing resources available to historically underserved communities.

Sustainable Healing Practices

Her work facilitating free workshops in rural areas on generational trauma, parenting skills, and goal-setting demonstrates commitment to community-based healing that extends beyond individual therapy. Her understanding that healing work affects everyone involved ensures "Return to the Source" maintains practices that protect both facilitators and participants from secondary trauma.

International Cultural Leadership

Her appointment as a Reference Group member for the Global Cultural Assembly at Berlin's Humboldt Forum represents international recognition of her expertise in addressing cultural erasure and colonial legacies. Her co-research on first responder stress published in Psychological Trauma: Theory, Research, Practice, and Policy demonstrates scientific rigor that ensures the lab's therapeutic approaches are grounded in evidence-based practice rather than merely good intentions.
