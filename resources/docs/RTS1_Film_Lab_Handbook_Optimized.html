Welcome Note

Programme Directors' Message

It is with great pleasure that we welcome you to this groundbreaking initiative designed to connect history, cinema, and community through the powerful medium of film. Over the next five days, we will embark on a journey that delves deep into Namibia's rich yet traumatic past, exploring its colonial history and its enduring impact on contemporary life.

This workshop is not so much about learning the technical aspects of filmmaking as it is about using film as a tool to exorcise trauma, to reclaim embodied histories, and to confront colonial ghosts that still linger in our societies.

We encourage you to engage fully with the Programme, participate in discussions, and let the landscapes, stories, and histories you encounter shape your creative process. This is more than just a film lab; it is a collective effort to return to our sources, understand our pasts, and chart new futures through the lens of cinema.

Meet the Facilitators

Expert Guidance for Transformative Learning

Dr. Nashilongweshipwe Mushaandja

Theoretical Architect & Cultural Methodologist

PhD in Performance Studies from University of Cape Town. Resident cultural worker at Owela Live Arts Collective. Specializes in African performance archives and public cultures of social movements. Lecturer in Drama and Theatre Arts at University of Namibia.

Khalid Shamis

Post-Production & Editing Specialist

Documentary director, producer, and editor with preference for editing. Doctoral fellow at Centre for Humanities Research, University of the Western Cape. Director of Rough Cut Lab Africa, supporting African independent documentaries.

Perivi Katjavivi

Programme Co-Director & Filmmaker

Distinguished Namibian filmmaker with films premiered at Berlinale, Rotterdam, and Busan. "Under the Hanging Tree" was Namibia's first Oscar submission. MA in African Cinema, PhD candidate in History at University of the Western Cape.

Fibby Kioria

Programme Co-Director & Strategic Producer

Founder of Mucii Pictures and co-founder of Manyatta Screenings. Over 30 short films produced through African film labs. Founding member of East African Screen Collective. Lives in Laikipia East, Kenya.

Merapi Gottlieb Kazombiaze

Community Historian & Cultural Guide

Herero historian and teacher from Okandjira, Namibia. Dedicated to preserving Herero Genocide memory. 38 years teaching experience, extensive historical projects, and connections with Germany for dialogue between past adversaries.
