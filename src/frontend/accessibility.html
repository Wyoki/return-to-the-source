Accessibility Statement

Our commitment to making our website and programs accessible to everyone

Our Commitment to Accessibility

Return to the Source Film Lab is committed to ensuring that our website and programs are accessible to everyone, including people with disabilities. We believe that access to cultural education and healing should be available to all, regardless of physical or cognitive abilities.

We strive to conform to Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standards and are continuously working to improve accessibility across all our digital platforms and physical programs.

Accessibility Features

Our website includes the following accessibility features:

Keyboard Navigation

All interactive elements can be accessed and operated using only the keyboard. Skip links allow users to jump directly to main content.

Screen Reader Support

Proper semantic HTML structure, ARIA labels, and alternative text for images ensure compatibility with screen readers.

Color and Contrast

High contrast color combinations meet WCAG AA standards. We support user preference for reduced motion and high contrast modes.

Responsive Design

Our website adapts to different screen sizes and can be zoomed up to 200% without loss of functionality.

Clear Typography

Large, readable fonts with adequate line spacing. Text can be resized without breaking the layout.

Focus Management

Clear focus indicators and logical tab order help users navigate efficiently with keyboards or assistive technologies.

Program Accessibility

Physical Programs

Our film labs and residencies are designed with accessibility in mind:

  • Wheelchair-accessible venues and transportation
  • Sign language interpretation available upon request
  • Flexible scheduling to accommodate different needs
  • Quiet spaces and sensory-friendly environments
  • Support for participants with diverse learning styles

Digital Programs

Online components of our programs include:

  • Captioned and transcribed video content
  • Accessible document formats
  • Screen reader compatible materials
  • Alternative formats for visual content

Standards and Guidelines

We follow internationally recognized accessibility standards:

  • WCAG 2.1 Level AA: Web Content Accessibility Guidelines
  • Section 508: U.S. federal accessibility standards
  • EN 301 549: European accessibility requirements
  • ISO 14289-1: PDF accessibility standards

Known Limitations

While we strive for full accessibility, some legacy content may have limitations:

  • Some older PDF documents may not be fully accessible
  • Third-party embedded content may not always meet our standards
  • Some complex data visualizations may require alternative formats

We are actively working to address these limitations and welcome feedback on areas for improvement.

Assistive Technologies

Our website is designed to work with:

  • Screen readers (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver, TalkBack)
  • Braille displays
  • Voice recognition software
  • Alternative input devices
  • Text-to-speech software

Feedback and Support

We welcome feedback on accessibility and are committed to continuous improvement. If you experience any difficulties accessing our content or have suggestions for improvement, please contact us.
