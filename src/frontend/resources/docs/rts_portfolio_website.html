Edition 1 Preview: Transforming Trauma into Creative Power

Our inaugural lab in Ovitoto, Namibia, brought together filmmakers from four African countries to engage with sites of colonial history through innovative archive methodologies. Participants worked directly with elder historians, exploring how landscape itself serves as archive, and creating collaborative films that honor memory while building contemporary creative capacity. The integration of trauma-informed care with artistic excellence proved that healing and high-quality creative work can be achieved simultaneously.
