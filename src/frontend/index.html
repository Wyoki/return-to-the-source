A groundbreaking Pan-African film lab that bridges history, healing, and future narratives. We redefine film education as both artistic expression and therapeutic intervention, creating a transformative approach to confronting colonial legacies and reclaiming African stories.

Our Revolutionary Approach

We combine cinematic expertise, industry development, and trauma-informed care to create a holistic approach that serves individual artistic development while contributing to broader community healing and cultural reclamation.

⧉

History as Material

We treat oral testimony, sacred geographies, and embodied ritual as primary sources equal to any colonial archive, transforming landscape into living classroom.

○

Healing as Protocol

A registered psychological counselor is embedded in every residency, ensuring that confronting genocide, slavery, or forced migration happens inside a container of care.

▶

Process as Product

We green-light experiences, not projects. If transformation happens but no film emerges, the lab is still scored as a success.

◎

Rotating Geographies

Each edition anchors in a different trauma landscape, building a lattice of Pan-African memory sites rather than a single branded campus.
