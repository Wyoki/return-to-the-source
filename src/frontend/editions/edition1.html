Edition 1 Impact Report

Five days in Namibia, reverberating across the continent.

Edition 1 Key Highlights

12 filmmakers

From Tanzania, Ethiopia, Kenya & Namibia; 50% women, 75% first-time lab attendees. Zero pitch decks, three collective short films.

1 embedded therapist

facilitated 7 debrief circles and 3 one-on-one sessions. Post-lab survey: 100% of participants said "psychological support was essential to my creative process".

3 genocide sites

(Ovitoto mission station, Hanging Tree, Okahandja cemetery) reclaimed as living sets—permission granted by local authorities and traditional leaders.

Community dividend

N$18,000 in local procurement (food, transport, accommodation) and 4 paid trainee positions for Ovitoto youth.

Institutional inversion

Goethe-Institut Namibia president attended sessions on German colonial activity—listening, not lecturing—modelling reparative cultural diplomacy.

Continental ripple

Alumni have since secured 5 development grants totalling €67,000, all citing RTS footage as proof-of-concept. Cape Verde edition already oversubscribed 3:1.
